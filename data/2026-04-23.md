Abstract: Cosmic neutrinos are among the most abundant fermions in the Universe, yet the values of their masses and chemical potentials remain uncertain. In this Letter, we present the first constraints on the total neutrino mass $M_ν$ and the neutrino asymmetry parameter $η^2$ derived from the mean galaxy pairwise peculiar velocity in the quasi-linear and nonlinear regimes. These results demonstrate that galaxy pairwise velocities provide an independent and sensitive probe of neutrino properties, opening a new avenue for testing neutrino physics with large-scale structure observations.
